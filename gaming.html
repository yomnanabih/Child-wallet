What is Gaming ?

Esports , short for electronic sports, is a form of competition using video games. Esports often takes the form of organized, multiplayer video game competitions, particularly between professional players, played individually or as teams. Players competing in a League of Legends tournament.

A professional gamer (in the purest sense of the term) is someone who competes at major esports competitions and takes their preparation every bit as seriously as a footballer or tennis player might do for their respective discipline

How To Start Gaming

Children can potentially earn money from gaming through various avenues, although it's essential to ensure that these activities are age-appropriate and supervised by adults. Here are some ways children can earn money from gaming:

  • Tournaments and Competitions

    Many gaming platforms host tournaments and competitions with cash prizes. Children with exceptional gaming skills can participate in these events to win money. However, parents should carefully monitor the terms and conditions of such competitions and ensure their child's safety and well-being.

  • Streaming and Content Creation

    Streaming gameplay on platforms like Twitch or creating gaming-related content on YouTube can be lucrative. Children can monetize their channels through ads, sponsorships, and donations from viewers. Parents should oversee their child's online activities and ensure they adhere to appropriate guidelines and regulations.

  • Game Testing

    Some companies hire game testers to provide feedback on new games. While this may not always involve direct monetary compensation, children can sometimes receive rewards such as free games, merchandise, or gift cards.

  • Game Development

    Children interested in coding and game development can create and sell their games. Platforms like Roblox and Minecraft offer opportunities for young developers to create and monetize their creations.
