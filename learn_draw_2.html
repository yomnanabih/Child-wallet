Your Second Step is Create a Portfolio

Explore online platforms where children can sell their artwork. Websites like Etsy, Redbubble, and Society6 allow artists to create their own online stores and sell prints, stickers, clothing, and other products featuring their artwork. Parents should supervise and assist with setting up these accounts as they often require individuals to be of a certain age to create accounts.

How to Create a Portfolio?

Creating a portfolio is an essential step for any artist, including children, to showcase their artwork effectively. Here's a step-by-step guide on how to create a portfolio:

  • Select Your Best Work

    Gather a selection of the child's best artwork. This should include a variety of pieces that demonstrate their skills, creativity, and range of interests. Choose artwork that showcases their strengths and reflects their unique style.

  • Organize Your Portfolio

    Decide how you want to organize the portfolio. You can arrange the artwork chronologically, by theme, medium, or any other method that makes sense for showcasing the child's work. Make sure the portfolio flows smoothly and is easy to navigate.

  • Choose a Presentation Format

    Decide on the format for presenting the portfolio. This could be a physical portfolio binder with printed copies of the artwork, a digital portfolio saved as a PDF or website, or a combination of both. Consider what will be most convenient for showcasing the child's work to potential clients or audiences.

  • Prepare High-Quality Images

    If creating a digital portfolio, ensure that the images of the artwork are of high quality. Use a good camera or scanner to capture clear, well-lit images of each piece. Pay attention to details such as color accuracy and composition.

  • Include Artist Statement and Bio

    Write a brief artist statement and biography to accompany the portfolio. The artist statement should provide insight into the child's artistic vision, inspiration, and goals. The biography should highlight key achievements, interests, and experiences related to art.

  • Add Contact Information

    Include contact information such as the child's name, email address, and any social media handles or website links where they can be reached. This makes it easy for potential clients or interested parties to get in touch.

  • Customize the Presentation

    Tailor the presentation of the portfolio to the child's personality and style. Consider adding a cover page or introduction that captures attention and sets the tone for the portfolio. Use design elements such as color schemes, fonts, and layouts that complement the artwork.

  • Review and Revise

    Review the portfolio carefully to ensure it accurately represents the child's skills and interests. Make any necessary revisions to improve the overall presentation and clarity of the portfolio.
